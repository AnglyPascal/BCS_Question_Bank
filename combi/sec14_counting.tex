

\section{Counting Problems}





\prob{https://artofproblemsolving.com/community/c6h127860p725458}{Hong Kong National MO 2002 P2}{}{In conference there $n>2$ mathematicians. Every two mathematicians communicate in one of the $n$ offical languages of the conference. For any three different offical languages the exists three mathematicians who communicate with each other in these three languages. Find all $n$ such that this is possible.}




\solu{Show a symmetrical construction for $n=2k+1$ and use counting to show that $n=2k$ doesn't work.}
